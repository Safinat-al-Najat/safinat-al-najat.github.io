> **No, 10th Muharram is a day of grieving. There are fake rumors regarding this day as stated in the hadith below:**


![[image-513.webp|418x601]]
![[image-511.webp|418x601]]

>**1 —** The majestic Sheikh, Abu Ja'far Muhammad bin Ali bin al-Husayn bin Musa bin Babawayh al-Qummi (may God be pleased with him) narrated to us, saying: Al-Husayn bin Ahmad bin Idris (may God be pleased with him) narrated to us, saying: My father narrated to us, from Muhammad ibn al-Husayn bin Abi al-Khattab, from Nasr bin Muzahim, from Umar bin Sa'd, from Arta'ah ibn Habib, from Fudayl al-Rassan, from Jabalah al-Makkiyyah, who said:

I heard Maytham al-Tammar (may God be pleased with him) saying:

> "By God, this nation will certainly kill the son of its Prophet in Muharram, when ten days of it have passed. And the enemies of God will certainly take that day as a day of blessing. Indeed, that is bound to happen; it has already preceded in the knowledge of God the Almighty, may His mention be exalted. I know this through a covenant entrusted to me by my master, the Commander of the Faithful [Ali] (blessings of God be upon him). He informed me that everything would weep for him [al-Husayn]—even the wild beasts in the wilderness, the whales in the oceans, the birds in the sky, and the sun, the moon, the stars, the sky, the earth, the believers among humans and jinn, all the angels of the heavens, [the angels] Ridwan and Malik, and the bearers of the Throne. And the sky will rain blood and ashes."

Then he said:

> "The curse of God is mandatory upon the killers of al-Husayn, peace be upon him, just as it became mandatory upon the polytheists who associate another god with God, and just as it became mandatory upon the Jews, the Christians, and the Magians."

Jabalah said: 

>I said to him: "O Maytham, how can the people take that day on which al-Husayn bin Ali (peace be upon them both) is killed as a day of blessing?"

Maytham wept, then said:

> "They will fabricate a hadith claiming that it is the day on which God accepted the repentance of Adam (peace be upon him), whereas God accepted Adam's repentance in [the month of] Dhu al-Hijjah.
> 
> They claim it is the day on which God accepted the repentance of David (peace be upon him), whereas God accepted his repentance in Dhu al-Hijjah.
> 
> They claim it is the day on which God brought Jonah (peace be upon him) out from the belly of the whale, whereas God brought him out from the belly of the whale in [the month of] Dhu al-Qa'dah.
> 
> They claim it is the day on which Noah's ark rested on Mount Judi, whereas it rested on Mount Judi on the eighteenth of Dhu al-Hijjah.
> 
> And they claim it is the day on which God parted the sea for the Children of Israel, whereas that took place in [the month of] Rabi' al-Awwal."

Then Maytham said:

> "O Jabalah, know that al-Husayn bin Ali (peace be upon them both) is the master of martyrs on the Day of Resurrection, and his companions excel over all other martyrs. O Jabalah, if you look at the sun and see it as red as fresh blood, then know that your master has been killed."

Jabalah said: 

>I went out one day and saw the sun on the walls as if they were dyed with safflower. Thereupon, I screamed and wept, and I said: 'By God, our master al-Husayn bin Ali (peace be upon them both) has been killed.'"

---

>**2 —** Ja'far bin Muhammad bin Masroor (may God be pleased with him) narrated to us, saying: Al-Husayn bin Muhammad bin Aamir narrated to us, from his uncle Abdullah bin Aamir, from Ibrahim bin Abi Mahmoud, who said: Imam al-Rida (peace be upon him) said:

> "Indeed, Muharram is a month in which the people of the Pre-Islamic Ignorance (_Jahiliyyah_) used to forbid fighting. Yet, our blood was deemed permissible in it, our sanctity was violated, our children and women were taken captive, our tents were set ablaze, our belongings were plundered, and the reverence of the Messenger of God (peace and blessings of God be upon him and his family) regarding our matter was not respected.
> 
> Indeed, the day of al-Husayn has ulcerated our eyelids, caused our tears to flow, and humiliated our beloved one in the land of affliction and calamity (_Karb and Bala_), bequeathing to us sorrow and affliction until the Day of Extinction. So let the weepers weep for the likes of al-Husayn, for indeed, weeping for him erases great sins."

Then he [Imam al-Rida] said:

> "Whenever the month of Muharram arrived, my father [Imam al-Kadhim] (blessings of God be upon him) was never seen laughing. Melancholy would overcome him until ten days of it had passed. When it was the tenth day, that day would be his day of tragedy, grief, and weeping, and he would say: 'This is the day on which al-Husayn (blessings of God be upon him) was killed.'"
