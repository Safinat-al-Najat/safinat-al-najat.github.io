Narrated from Ibn Abbass:
 
"I saw the Prophet ﷺ in a dream at midday, disheveled and covered in dust. He had with him a glass bottle containing blood which he was collecting, or tracking something in it. I said: 'O Messenger of Allah, what is this?' He replied: **'The blood of Al-Husayn and his companions; I have not stopped collecting it since today.'**"

Ammar said: "We memorized that day, and we found that he Al-Husayn had been killed on that very day."

[Musnad Ahmad 2165](https://quranohadith.com/musnad-ahmed/2165)


![[image-44.webp|418x601]]![[image-42.webp|418x601]]![[image-43.webp|418x601]]

"Abu Ammar Shaddad bin Abdullah narrates that Umm al-Fadl bint al-Harith came to the service of the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) and said: 'O Messenger of Allah (ﷺ), I saw a terrifying dream last night.' He (ﷺ) asked: 'What was it?' She replied: 'It was very terrifying.' He (ﷺ) asked again: 'What was it?' She said: 'I saw as if a piece of your body was cut off and placed in my lap.'

The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: 'You have seen a good dream. (Its interpretation is that) Fatima will give birth to a son, and he will be placed in your lap, God willing.'

Consequently, Hussain  was born to Fatima , and according to the word of the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ), he was placed in my lap. One day, I went to the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) and placed him in his lap. After a while, when I turned my attention, I saw tears flowing from the eyes of the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ). I asked: 'O Prophet of Allah (ﷺ), may my parents be sacrificed for you, what is the matter? (Why are you crying?)'

He (ﷺ) replied: 'Jibril (Gabriel) came to me and informed me that my Ummah (nation) will martyr this son of mine.' I asked (in great surprise): 'This one?' He (ﷺ) replied: 'Yes. And he (Jibril) brought me red soil from his (place of martyrdom).'"

[Mustadrak Al Hakim 4818](https://al-hadees.com/mustadrak/4818)

Muhammad ibn Ubayd narrated to us, Sharhabeel ibn Mudrik narrated to us, from Abdullah ibn Nujayy, from his father, that he traveled with Ali (may Allah be pleased with him)—and he was the carrier of Ali's water vessel for purification—when they were heading toward Siffin. When they reached Nineveh, Ali called out: "Be patient, O Abu Abdullah! Be patient, O Abu Abdullah, by the bank of the Euphrates!"

I asked: "And why is that?"

Ali replied: "I entered upon the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) one day and his eyes were overflowing with tears. I said: 'O Prophet of Allah, has someone angered you? Why are your eyes overflowing?'

He said: 'No, but Jibril (Gabriel) left me just now and informed me that Al-Husayn will be killed by the bank of the Euphrates.'

The Prophet continued: 'He (Jibril) said: "Would you like to smell some of his soil (from the place of his death)?" I said: "Yes." So he stretched out his hand, took a handful of soil, and gave it to me. I could not help but let my eyes overflow with tears.'"

[Musnad Ahmad 648](https://quranohadith.com/musnad-ahmed/648)

Some may question these narrations as da'if — but according to the principles of Usool al-Hadith, when multiple da'if narrations share the same meaning and their weakness stems from an unknown narrator or similar minor reasons, they collectively elevate to the grade of **Hasan li-ghayrihi**. The sheer number of such narrations on this matter makes the point well-established.


Aqa Muhammad ﷺ himself wept for Hussain — so mourning on the 10th of Muharram is not a bid'ah. It is a Sunnah.
