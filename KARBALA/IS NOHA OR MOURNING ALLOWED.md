
> **Some people claim that it is not allowed to mourn over the deceased, while it can be observed from the life of the Aqa Muhammad ﷺ.**


# 1st Evidence

![[image-562.webp|418x601]]![[image-560.webp|418x601]]![[image-561.webp|418x601]]

> **498/4900 –** Al-Hakim Abu Abdullah Muhammad bin Abdullah Al-Hafiz narrated to us by dictation in Al-Muharram of the year 403 AH: Abu Al-Husayn Muhammad bin Ahmed bin Tamim Al-Qantari informed me in Baghdad, Ubayd bin Sharik narrated to us, Abu Salih Al-Farra' narrated to us, Abu Ishaq Al-Fazari narrated to us, from Abu Hammad Al-Hanafi, from Abdullah bin Muhammad bin Aqil, who said: I heard Jabir bin Abdullah (may Allah be pleased with them both) say: On the day of Uhud, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ missed Hamzah when the people returned from the battle. He [Jabir] said: A man then said, "I saw him by that tree saying: 'I am the lion of Allah and the lion of His Messenger. O Allah, I declare myself innocent before You of what these people have brought'—referring to Abu Sufyan and his companions—'and I offer my excuse to You for what these people have done by retreating.'" So the Messenger of Allah ﷺ walked toward him, and when he saw his forehead, he wept. And when he saw how he [Hamzah] had been mutilated, **he sobbed out loud with a sharp gasp**, then he said: "Is there no shroud?" So a man from the Ansar stood up and threw a garment over him. Jabir said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ then said: "The leader of the martyrs in the sight of Allah the Exalted on the Day of Resurrection is Hamzah."

[Mustadrak Al Hakim 4900](https://hadithunlocked.com/hakim:4900)

# 2nd Evidence

![[image-563.webp|418x601]]![[image-564.webp|418x601]]![[image-565.webp|418x601]]

> from Ibn Umar: "When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ returned from [the battle of] Uhud, the women of the Ansar began weeping for those who had been killed from among their husbands. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "But Hamzah has no women weeping for him." He [Ibn Umar] said: Then he slept, and when he woke up, they were still weeping. 
> 
> He (Ahmad Ibn Hanbal) said: "So to this day, when they (people) weep, they mourn [lament] for Hamzah (first)."

> **(4984)** Its chain of narration is authentic (Sahih)

Musnad Ahmad 4984

It is corroborated by the 3rd Evidence.

---

# 3rd Evidence

![[image-567.webp|418x601]]![[image-566.webp|418x601]]

> **And Imam Ahmad documented its chain of narration [4/40] and said:** Zayd bin Al-Hubab narrated to us, Usamah bin Zayd narrated to me, Nafi' narrated to me, from Ibn Umar: When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ returned from [the battle of] Uhud, the women of the Ansar began weeping for those who had been killed from among their husbands. The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "But Hamzah has no women weeping for him." He [Ibn Umar] said: Then he slept, and when he woke up, they were still weeping. 
> 
> He (Ahmad Ibn Hanbal) said: "So to this day, when they (people) weep, they mourn [lament] for Hamzah (first)."

> And this is according to the criteria of [Imam] Muslim.

**So, Ibn Kathir also Authenticated this report.**
