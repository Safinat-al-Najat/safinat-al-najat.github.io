# First Evidence

![[image-551.webp|418x601]]![[image-545.webp|418x601]]![[image-547.webp|418x601]]
> **2846 –** Ali bin Abdul-Aziz narrated to us, Az-Zubair bin Bakkar narrated to us, Muhammad bin Al-Dahhak bin Uthman Al-Hizami narrated to me from his father, who said: Al-Husayn bin Ali (may Allah be pleased with them both) set out for Kufa, rejecting the rule of Yazid bin Muawiyah. So Yazid bin Muawiyah wrote to Ubaydullah bin Ziyad—who was his governor over Iraq—saying: "It has reached me that Husayn has marched toward Kufa. Your time out of all times, your land out of all lands, and you out of all governors have been tested with him. At this juncture, a person is either freed or returns to being a slave, just as slaves are enslaved." Consequently, Ubaydullah bin Ziyad killed him and sent his head to Yazid. When it was placed before him, Yazid quoted the verse of Al-Husayn bin Al-Humam:
> 
>  "We split the skulls of men who were beloved to us, yet they were more defiant and unjust [towards us]."

> **2846 –** He said in _Al-Majma'_ (9/193): Its narrators are trustworthy, except that Al-Dahhak did not witness the actual event.

This _irsal_ is not an issue since it's a historical account that concerns a letter (which is physically transmitted not orally).

It is also corroborated by the Fifth Evidence given in this article.

# Fourth Evidence

![[image-554.webp|418x601]]![[image-555.webp|418x601]]![[image-556.webp|418x601]]

> And Bakr bin Hammad narrated to me, he said: Zuhayr bin Abbad Ar-Ruwasi narrated to us, he said: Abu Omar Al-San'ani narrated to us, from Haram bin Uthman, who said: The head of Al-Husayn bin Ali bin Abi Talib was brought and placed before Yazid bin Muawiyah bin Abi Sufyan. He began striking his face with a stick, inserting it into his mouth and eyes. So Zayd bin Arqam said: "Raise your stick from its place!" Yazid asked: "And why?" He replied: "Because I saw the mouth of the Messenger of Allah, ﷺ, in that very place." Yazid said: "You are an old man who has become senile." So Zayd stepped down from the couch, on which he had been sitting alongside Yazid, and said: "The real wonder is at this! I bear witness that I saw the Prophet, ﷺ, seat him on his left thigh, place his hand on his head, and say: 'O Allah, I entrust him and the righteous believers to Your care.' So how well have you guarded the trust of the Messenger of Allah, ﷺ?"

> Hizam bin Uthman said: I was narrated this tradition in Iraq. When I arrived in Medina, Sa'id bin Mu'adh and others narrated to me that they were present at that moment when Zayd bin Arqam said this to Yazid.

# Fifth Evidence

![[image-559.webp|418x601]]![[image-557.webp|418x601]]![[image-558.webp|418x601]]

> **2806 –** Abu Al-Zinba' Ruh bin Al-Faraj Al-Masri narrated to us, Yahya bin Bukayr narrated to us, Al-Layth narrated to me, saying: Al-Husayn bin Ali (may Allah be pleased with them both) refused to surrender, so they fought him. They killed him, his two sons, and his companions who fought alongside him at a place called Al-Taff. Then, Ali bin Al-Husayn, Fatima bint Al-Husayn, and Sukaynah bint Al-Husayn were taken to Ubaydullah bin Ziyad. Ali at that time was a youth who had reached puberty. He [Ibn Ziyad] sent them to Yazid bin Muawiyah. Yazid ordered Sukaynah to be placed behind his couch so that she would not see the head of her father and her relatives, while Ali bin Al-Husayn (may Allah be pleased with them both) was in shackles. The head was placed down, and Yazid began striking the front teeth of Al-Husayn (may Allah be pleased with him), reciting: 
> 
> _"We split the skulls of men who were beloved to us, yet they were more defiant and unjust [towards us]."_ 
> 
> Thereupon, Ali bin Al-Husayn (may Allah be pleased with him) replied, reciting: 
> 
> _"No disaster strikes upon the earth or among yourselves except that it is in a register before We bring it into being indeed that is easy for Allah."_
> 
> It weighed heavily on Yazid that Ali countered his poetry by reciting a verse from the Book of Allah, Mighty and Majestic. So Yazid responded by reciting: 
> 
> _"And whatever affliction befalls you, it is because of what your hands have earned; and He pardons much."_ 
> 
> Ali (may Allah be pleased with him) then said: 
> 
> "By Allah, if the Messenger of Allah ﷺ were to see us bound in shackles, he would surely want to release us from them." 
> 
> Yazid said: "You speak the truth," and ordered them released from the shackles. Ali then said: 
> 
> "And if we were standing before the Messenger of Allah ﷺ from a distance, he would surely want to bring us closer." 
> 
> Yazid said: "You speak the truth," and ordered them brought closer. Then, Fatima and Sukaynah began craning their necks to catch a glimpse of their father's head, while Yazid kept stretching upward from his seat to conceal the head of their father from their view. He then gave orders regarding them; provisions were prepared, their affairs were set right, and they were sent forth to Medina.
