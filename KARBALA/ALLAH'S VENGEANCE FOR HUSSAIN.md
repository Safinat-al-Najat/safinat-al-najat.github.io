Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with them both

**Core Text:**

> The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said:  
> "Allah the Almighty revealed to Muhammad ﷺ: 'Indeed, I killed seventy thousand for the blood of John the son of Zechariah (Yahya bin Zakariyya), and I will surely kill seventy thousand and seventy thousand for the blood of your daughter's son (Al-Husayn).'"
> 
> This is the wording of the hadith according to Al-Shafi'i. In the narration of Al-Qadi Abu Bakr bin Kamil, it reads: "Indeed, I killed for the blood of John the son of Zechariah, and I will surely kill for the blood of your daughter's son."

**Author's Comment:**

> "This is a hadith with an authentic chain of transmission (_sahih al-isnad_), but they [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] did not record it."


[Mustadrak Al Hakim 4822](https://al-hadees.com/mustadrak/4822)

Note: This is not the only chain that al-Hakim provides, he gives 5 different chains back to Abu Nu’aym. Some may take issue with Habib b. Abi Thabit in the chain as he is recorded as being a mudalis, but Dr. Abdullah b. Abdul-Rahman al-Si’ad refutes this matter in his [Minhaj al-Mutaqadamin Fi al-Tadlis, pg. 123 - 126](https://static.wixstatic.com/media/436d7d_9a5725d8299a42acb1e1bf410885c037~mv2.webp):

**"Abū Bakr ibn ʿAyyāsh reported from al-Aʿmash that he (Habib) used to say: If a man narrated to me from you, I would not mind attributing it to you - meaning that he would omit the intermediary."**

**There are a few points to mention [regarding Habib]:**

**First: Those who described him with tadlis did not mention that he did it much; rather, in Ibn Hibban’s statement "there is tadlis in him" is an indication of its rarity.**

**Second: That their description of him with tadlis makes it more likely that what is meant by it is irsal, because most of the scholars who spoke about him only spoke about his** **_irsal,_** **especially from Urwah b. al-Zubayr, and he only has two hadiths from him. [...]** 

**Thirdly: The phrase “meaning, he omitted the intermediary” is al-Ḥāfiẓ Ibn Ḥajar’s interpretation of Ḥabīb’s words. This interpretation is possible, but not definitive, it could also mean, for example, that he narrated it without going back to al-Aʿmash to verify it.**

**In sum, the total of what has been mentioned does not indicate that he practiced tadlis frequently, but rather that he was little involved in tadlis, as appears from his biographies and narrations. And Allah, exalted is He, knows best.**

**And the answer to these questions is not found by returning to the writings of the later scholars, but rather by referring back to the words of the early authorities, examining their expressions carefully, and tracing the narrations of the one described with tadlīs. In this way, the researcher is safeguarded from error, by the help and power of Allah.**
