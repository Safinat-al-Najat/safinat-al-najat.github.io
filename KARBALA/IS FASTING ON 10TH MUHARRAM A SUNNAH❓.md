
So, today many people say that Fasting on 10th of Muharram is a continued sunnah and a highly recommended sunnah of Muhammad ﷺ.

Lets break it down:


# 1st Hadith

![[image-594.webp|418x601]]![[image-595.webp|418x601]]
> **1892** — Musaddad narrated to us: Isma'il narrated to us, from Ayyub, from Nafi', from Ibn 'Umar (may Allah be pleased with them both), who said:
> 
> "The Prophet ﷺ fasted on _'Ashura'_ (the 10th of Muharram) and ordered [the Muslims] to fast on it. But when [the fasting of the month of] Ramadan was made obligatory, it was left [optional]. 'Abdullah [Ibn 'Umar] would not fast on that day unless it coincided with his [routine] fasting."

---

# 2nd Hadith
![[image-597.webp|418x601]]![[image-596.webp|418x601]]

> **[2648] 122 - (1127)** It was narrated that 'Abdur-Rahmân bin Yazîd said:
> 
> "Al-Ash'ath bin Qais entered upon 'Abdullâh when he was eating his lunch. He said: 'O Abû Muhammad, come and eat.' He said: 'Isn't today 'Âshûrâ'?' He said: 'Do you know what the day of 'Âshûrâ' is?' He said: 'What is it?' He said: 'It is a day that the Messenger of Allâh ﷺ used to fast before (fasting during) the month of Ramadân was enjoined. When the month of Ramadân was enjoined, it was abandoned.'"
> 
> Abû Kuraib said: "He abandoned it."
