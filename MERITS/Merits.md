In Islam, **merit** or **virtue** refers to spiritual excellence and closeness to God, measured by **piety and good deeds**, not by worldly status.

Key aspects:

- **Taqwa (Piety) is the highest merit:** The Quran states that the most honorable person is the most righteous, regardless of race or wealth.
    
- **Good deeds matter:** Virtue is earned through sincere worship, honesty, charity, and good character.
    
- **Intention is key:** A deed's value depends on the intention behind it.
    
- **Knowledge is virtuous:** Seeking knowledge is considered a path to Paradise.
    
- **Following the Prophet's example:** Emulating his character is the ultimate model of virtue.
