[Sahih Bukhari 836](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:836)

The background of the hadith is that it was raining, the mud got wet and Muhammad ﷺ was still prostrating at that area. So, there is no problem with mud or wet mud too.

But the Praying Mat is an innovation. It is better to avoid prayer mat and praying directly on earth or if using a prayer mat use dirt to perform sujood on.

In Majmoo Al Fatawa by Ibn Taymiyyah he supports placing turbah or dirt or stones for prayer instead of prayer mat.

![[image-424.webp|418x601]]![[image-427.webp|418x601]]![[image-426.webp|418x601]]

> **And he was asked about someone who spreads a prayer rug in the congregational mosque and prays upon it: Is what he did an innovation (_bid'ah_) or not?"**

**Page 163 (Top Section)**

> "He answered: Praise be to Allah, Lord of the Worlds. As for praying on a prayer rug in a manner where the worshipper explicitly seeks out doing so, this was not the Sunnah of the predecessors (_Salaf_) among the _Muhajirun_ (Emigrants) and the _Ansar_ (Helpers), nor those who followed them in righteousness during the era of the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him. Rather, they used to pray in his mosque directly on the ground; none of them took a specific prayer rug exclusively to pray on.
> 
> It was narrated that when Abd al-Rahman ibn Mahdi arrived in Medina, he spread out a prayer rug, so [Imam] Malik ordered him to be imprisoned. It was said to Malik: 'He is Abd al-Rahman ibn Mahdi!' Malik replied: 'Do you not know that spreading a prayer rug in our mosque is an innovation (_bid'ah_)?'"

**Page 163 (Bottom) & Page 164 (Top Section)**

> "And in the _Sahih_, from **Abu Sa'id al-Khudri**, regarding the narration of the Prophet’s (_peace and blessings of Allah be upon him_) seclusion (_I'tikaf_), he said: 'We observed _I'tikaf_ with the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him...' He mentioned the narration, and in it he said: 'Whoever observed _I'tikaf_, let him return to his place of seclusion, for I was shown this night, and I saw myself prostrating in water and mud.'
> 
> At the end of it, he said: 'I indeed saw—meaning on the morning of the twenty-first—the traces of water and mud on his nose and the tip of his nose.' This clearly shows that his prostration was on mud. His mosque was roofed with palm branches through which the rain leaked, so his mosque was made of the natural ground."

**Page 164 (Middle Section)**

> "And sometimes they would place pebbles in it, as recorded in _Sunan Abi Dawud_ from Abdallah ibn al-Harith, who said: I asked **Ibn Umar**—may Allah be pleased with them both—about the pebbles that were in the mosque, and he said: 'It rained upon us one night, and the ground became wet. So a man started bringing pebbles in his garment and spreading them beneath him. When the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, finished the prayer, he said: "How excellent this is!"'"

**Page 164 (Bottom Section)**

> "And in _Sunan Abi Dawud_ as well, from Abu Badr Shuja' ibn al-Walid, from Sharik, from Abu Husayn, from Abu Salih, from **Abu Hurayrah**, who said: (Abu Badr said: I think he attributed it directly to the Prophet, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, who said): 'The pebble implores the one who removes it from the mosque.'
> 
> For this reason, in the _Sunan_ and the _Musnad_, from **Abu Dharr**, he said: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said: 'When one of you stands for prayer, he should not wipe away the pebbles, for mercy is facing him.' And in a wording in _Musnad Ahmad_, he said: 'I asked the Prophet, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, about everything, until I asked him about wiping...'"
