> **Taraweeh** is a special night prayer performed only during the month of **Ramadan**. It consists of several units of prayer (_rak'ahs_) and is characterized by long recitations of the Quran.


![[image-284.png|443]]
![[image-285.png|440x688]]

The translation has been done wrong and has mistakes in the scan above. Following is the correct translation:

> **731 —** 'Abd al-A'la bin Hammad narrated to us, he said: Wuhaib narrated to us, he said: Musa ibn 'Uqbah narrated to us, from Salim Abi al-Nadr, from Busr bin Sa'id, from Zaid bin Thabit:
> 
> That the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) made a small room—the narrator said: "I think he said: made of a mat"—in Ramadan, and he prayed in it for a few nights. Then, some people from his companions prayed along with his prayer. When he became aware of them, he sat down. Then he came out to them and said:
> 
> **"I have seen and understood what you did. O people, pray in your houses, for the best prayer of a person is in his house, except for the obligatory prayer."**


[Sahih Bukhari 731](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:731)

> From **Ubayy bin Ka‘b**:

The Main Text (Matn)

> That **‘Umar (bin al-Khattab)** ordered Ubayy (bin Ka‘b) to lead the people in prayer during Ramadan.
> 
> ‘Umar said: **"The people fast during the day but do not recite (the Qur'an) well. If only you would recite the Qur'an to them at night."**
> 
> Ubayy replied: **"O Commander of the Faithful! This is something that has not been done before."**
> 
> ‘Umar responded: **"I know, but it is better."**
> 
> So, Ubayy led them in **twenty rak'ahs** of prayer.
