
## Halal and How It Is Determined in Islam

**Halal** (حلال) means "permissible" in Arabic — anything Allah has allowed for believers in terms of food, drink, actions, business, and daily conduct. Its opposite is **Haram** (حرام), meaning forbidden.

---

## How It Is Determined

Islamic scholars determine halal and haram through four main sources:

**The Quran** — the primary source, containing direct commands and prohibitions from Allah. For example, pork, alcohol, and blood are explicitly forbidden in the Quran.

**Sunnah and Hadith** — the sayings and actions of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), which clarify and expand upon Quranic rulings.

In Islamic law the default ruling for all things is halal unless explicitly stated otherwise. Prohibition requires clear evidence from scripture. This makes Islam's framework practical only a defined set of things are forbidden, while everything else remains permitted.
