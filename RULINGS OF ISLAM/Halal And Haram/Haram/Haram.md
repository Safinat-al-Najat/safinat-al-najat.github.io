**Haram** (حرام) means "forbidden" in Arabic — anything Allah has explicitly prohibited for believers. Engaging in haram is considered a sin that carries moral and spiritual consequences. Its opposite is **Halal** (حلال), meaning permissible.
## How It Is Determined

**The Quran** — the highest authority. Explicit Quranic verses establish the clearest prohibitions, leaving no room for debate.

**Sunnah and Hadith** — the Prophet's (ﷺ) sayings and actions clarify prohibitions not fully detailed in the Quran.

Haram requires clear and explicit evidence from scripture. Scholars follow the rule that **doubt does not establish prohibition** — if evidence is unclear or weak, the default remains permissibility. This ensures the law remains just and not unnecessarily burdensome upon believers.
