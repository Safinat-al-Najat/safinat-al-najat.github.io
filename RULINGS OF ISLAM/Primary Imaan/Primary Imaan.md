
## Primary Imaan (Faith) in Islam

**Imaan** (إيمان) means belief, faith, and conviction in the heart. Primary Imaan refers to the **fundamental articles of faith** that every Muslim must believe in — the core pillars upon which all of Islam rests.

**Belief in Allah** — that there is only one God, unique and without partners or equals.

**Belief in the Angels** — that Allah created angels from light, who carry out His commands and never disobey Him.

**Belief in the Divine Books** — the scriptures revealed by Allah, including the Torah, Psalms, Gospel, and the final and preserved Quran.

**Belief in the Prophets and Messengers** — that Allah sent prophets to guide humanity, beginning with Adam and sealed by Muhammad (ﷺ).

**Belief in the Day of Judgement** — that every soul will be resurrected and held accountable for its deeds.
