In Islam, **major historical events** are not just random occurrences. They are understood as **signs of God's power, lessons for humanity, and tests of faith.**

Key meanings:

- **Divine Lessons:** Events like the flood of Noah or the destruction of past nations serve as warnings and reminders of God's justice.
    
- **Tests of Faith:** Events like the battles faced by Prophet Muhammad (e.g., Badr, Uhud) were tests of patience, obedience, and trust in God.
    
- **Turning Points:** Events like the **Hijra** (migration to Medina) and the **conquest of Mecca** mark major shifts that strengthened the Muslim community and established justice.
    
- **Fulfillment of Promises:** Events like the **splitting of the moon** or the victory after hardship are seen as proofs of prophethood and God's support for believers.
    
- **Moral Guidance:** The stories of prophets and past communities teach what to emulate and what to avoid.
