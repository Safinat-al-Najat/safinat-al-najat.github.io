

***Opposition forces:***
Following their humiliating defeat at the **Battle of Badr** a year earlier, the Quraysh sought to restore their prestige and secure their trade routes to Syria, which the Muslims had been intercepting. Led by **Abu Sufyan**, they mobilized an army of 3,000 men—including a significant cavalry led by **Khalid ibn al-Walid**—to march on Medina.


***The Forces of Haq:***
Prophet Muhammad ﷺ led a smaller force of approximately 700 men to the slopes of **Mount Uhud**, located north of Medina. 

- **The Archer's Hill:** He strategically positioned **50 archers** on a nearby hill (Jabal al-Rumat) to guard the army's rear and prevent a cavalry ambush.
- **The Command:** His explicit order to the archers was to **never leave their posts**, regardless of whether the Muslims were winning or losing, even if they saw "birds snatching up" the soldiers.

Initially, the Muslims gained the upper hand, forcing the Meccan lines to retreat.  Seeing the Meccans flee, the majority of the archers (approximately 40) mistakenly believed the battle was over and abandoned their posts to collect **war booty**. Khalid ibn al-Walid seized this opening, leading his cavalry around the mountain to attack the Muslims from behind.
