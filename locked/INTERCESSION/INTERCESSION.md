# Hadith Evidence

## Hadith of Uthman Bin Hunayf

### Muajam Al Kabir Al Tabarani 8311

![[image-588.webp|418x601]]![[image-586.webp|418x601]]![[image-587.webp|418x601]]

> That a man used to frequent [visiting] Uthman bin ‘Affan (may Allah be pleased with him) for a need of his, but ‘Uthman would not pay attention to him nor look into his need. So the man met Ibn Hunayf and complained about that to him.
>
>‘Uthman bin Hunayf said to him: 'Go to the place of ablution (_Al-Mida'ah_) and perform ablution, then go to the mosque and pray two units of prayer (_rak'ah_) in it, and then say:
>
>> _"O Allah, I ask You and turn to You by means of our Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, the Prophet of Mercy. O Muhammad, I turn by means of you to my Lord so that my need may be fulfilled for me,"_
>
> and then state your need. Afterward, leave until I go out with you.'

### Mustadrak Al Hakim 1180

![[image-590.webp|418x601]]![[image-589.webp|418x601]]
> from 'Uthman bin Hunayf:
> 
> That a blind man came to the Prophet ﷺ and said: 'Supplicate to Allah to heal me.'  
> He ﷺ replied: 'If you wish, I will delay it [for the Hereafter] and that is better, and if you wish, I will supplicate.'  
> The man said: 'Supplicate to Him.'
> 
> So he [the Prophet] ordered him to perform ablution and do it well, pray two units of prayer (_rak'ah_), and supplicate with this supplication, saying:
> 
> > _'O Allah, I ask You and turn to You by means of Your Prophet Muhammad, the Prophet of Mercy. O Muhammad, I turn by means of you to my Lord regarding this need of mine so that it may be fulfilled for me. O Allah, accept his intercession for me.'_"

> **Hakim's Verdict:**
>
>"This is an authentic (_Sahih_) Hadith according to the conditions of the two Sheikhs [Al-Bukhari and Muslim], though they did not record it."

> "1180 - He [Al-Dhahabi] said in _Al-Talkhis_: According to their conditions [meaning Al-Bukhari and Muslim]."
