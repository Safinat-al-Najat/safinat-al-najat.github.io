Today a question arises, if it is allowed to ask help from anyone other than Allah. So, let's look into it.

# ASKING HELP FROM ANGELS IN DESERT

## Al Musanaf Al Ahadith Al Asaar 29819

![[image-598.webp|418x601]]![[image-600.webp|418x601]]
> **(131) What a man says if his mount or camel bolts while traveling**
> 
> **29819 —** Yazid bin Harun narrated to us, saying: Muhammad bin Ishaq informed us, from Aban bin Salih, from 'Ata' bin Abi Rabah, from Ibn 'Abbas, that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:
> 
> _"If the mount or camel of one of you bolts in a desert land where he sees no one, let him say: 'O servants of Allah, help me!' For indeed, he will be answered."_

---

## Muajam Al Kabir Al Tabarani 10518

![[image-601.webp|418x601]]
![[image-603.webp|418x601]]

> **10518 —** Ibrahim bin Na'ilah al-Asbahani narrated to us, saying: Al-Hasan bin 'Umar bin Shaqiq narrated to us, saying: Ma'ruf bin Hassan al-Samarqandi narrated to us, from Sa'id bin Abi 'Arubah, from Qatadah, from 'Abdullah bin Buraydah, from 'Abdullah bin Mas'ud, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:
> 
> _"If the mount of one of you bolts in a desert land, let him call out: 'O servants of Allah, restrain it for me!' For indeed, Allah has those present on the earth who will restrain it for you."_

## Muajam Al Kabir Al Tabarani 290

![[image-604.webp|418x601]]![[image-605.webp|418x601]]![[image-606.webp|418x601]]

> **(290)** Al-Husayn bin Ishaq al-Tustari narrated to us, saying: Ahmad bin Yahya al-Sufi narrated to us, saying: Abdur-Rahman bin Sahl narrated to us, saying: My father narrated to us, from Abdullah bin Isa, from Zayd bin Ali, from Utbah bin Ghazwan, from the Prophet ﷺ, who said:
> 
> _"If one of you loses something or requires help while in a land where he has no companion, let him say: 'O servants of Allah, help me! O servants of Allah, help me!' For indeed, Allah has servants whom we do not see. And indeed, this has been tested and experienced."_

## Al Bahar Al Zakhar Musnad Bazar 4922

![[image-609.webp|418x601]]![[image-611.webp|418x601]]
> **4922 —** Musa bin Ishaq narrated to us, saying: Minjab bin al-Harith narrated to us, saying: Hatim bin Isma'il narrated to us, from Usamah bin Zayd, from Aban bin Salih, from Mujahid, from Ibn 'Abbas (may Allah be pleased with them both), that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:
> 
> _"Indeed, Allah has angels on the earth, other than the guardian angels, who write down whatever leaves fall from the trees. So if one of you experiences a sudden impediment (or lameness/mishap) in a desert land, let him call out: 'Help me, O servants of Allah!'"_

> **(3)** Al-Bayhaqi recorded it in _Shu'ab al-Iman_ (1/183), No. (167), through the chain of Usamah bin Zayd from Aban, with this text.
> 
> Al-Haythami also mentioned it in _Al-Majma'_ (10/132), attributing it to Al-Tabarani, and stated: _"Its narrators are trustworthy (thiqat)."_

**BLUE HIGHLIGHTED SECTIONS:**

> **Chapter on what one says if his mount bolts**
> 
> We narrated in the book of Ibn al-Sunni from 'Abdullah bin Mas'ud (may Allah be pleased with him), from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, who said: _"If the mount of one of you bolts in a desert land, let him call out: 'O servants of Allah, restrain [it]! O servants of Allah, restrain [it]!' For indeed, Allah (the Mighty and Majestic) has a responder on the earth who will restrain it [for you]."_

> I (Author[Al-Nawawi]) say: One of our senior scholars in knowledge narrated to me that his mount—which I believe was a mule—bolted from him. He knew this Hadith, so he spoke these words, and Allah stopped it for them immediately.
> 
> I was also once with a group when an animal belonging to us bolted, and they were unable to catch it. I spoke these words, and it stopped immediately without any cause other than these words.

---

**GREEN HIGHLIGHTED SECTIONS:**

> And Al-Mirak said: _"Some scholars declared the transmitters of this Hadith to be trustworthy (thiqat), deeming it a good (hasan) tradition needed by the traveler. It has been narrated from some of our Mashayikh (teachers) that it is highly effective, and success is linked to it."_
> 
> Perhaps he intended that it becomes good (_hasan_) by virtue of its corroborating pathways.

> Al-Hafiz [Ibn Hajar] said: _"The Hadith of 'Utbah has a supporting witness (_shahid_) from the Hadith of Ibn 'Abbas, that the Prophet ﷺ said: 'Indeed, Allah (Exalted be He) has angels on the earth, other than the guardian angels, who write down whatever leaves fall from the trees. So if one of you experiences a sudden impediment in a desert land, let him call out: Help me, O servants of Allah!''"_
> 
> This is a good (_hasan_) tradition with an excellent chain of transmission (_hasan al-isnad_).

## Masail Ahmad Bin Hanbal (Abdullah Ibn Ahmad) 912
![[image-621.webp|418x601]]![[image-622.webp|418x601]]
> **912 —** He [Abdullah] narrated to us, saying: I heard my father [Imam Ahmad bin Hanbal] say:
> 
> _"I performed Hajj five times—two of them riding and three walking, or two walking and three riding. During one Hajj, I lost my way while I was walking, so I began to say: 'O servants of Allah, guide us to the path!' I kept saying that until I found the path,"_ or as my father said.

So, Ahmad Bin Hanbal himself accepted this hadith and also acted upon it.

---

![[image-625.webp|418x601]]![[image-624.webp|418x601]]![[image-623.webp|418x601]]
> **656 —** _(“If one of you loses something, or one of you desires help while in a land where there is no companion, let him say: ‘O servants of Allah, help me! O servants of Allah, help me!’ For indeed, Allah has servants whom we do not see.”)_

> This... and it appears that the Hadith of Ibn 'Abbas, which Al-Hafiz [Ibn Hajar] declared good (_hasan_), was deemed strong by Imam Ahmad because he acted upon it. His son 'Abdullah said in _Al-Masa'il_ (217):
> 
> _“I heard my father say: I performed Hajj five times—two of them [riding] and three walking, or two walking and three riding. I lost my way during one Hajj while I was walking, so I began to say: ‘O servants of Allah, guide us to the path!’ I kept saying that until I found the path.”_ Or as my father said.
> 
> And Al-Bayhaqi recorded it in _Al-Shu'ab_ (2/455) and Ibn 'Asakir (1/72/3) via the route of 'Abdullah with an **authentic chain of transmission (_isnad sahih_)**.

# CONCLUSION:

Consequently, we have demonstrated that **Ahmad bin Hanbal** and **Al-Tabarani** acted upon this practice, while other authorities like **Mirak** and **Hafiz Ibn Hajar** deemed it effective. Given that **Ibn Taymiyyah** also adhered to the **Hanbali school** of jurisprudence, this perspective should logically align with his framework as well (as an independent deduction).

Furthermore, numerous alternative sources explicitly classify this narration as _hasan_ (sound), and I have successfully demonstrated that it possesses multiple independent chains of transmission. 

Finally, we can deduce that Allah has granted these angels authority to assist his creation. Ultimately, the source of all power is Allah, and all omnipotence belongs strictly to Him. However, seeking assistance from an unseen entity other than Allah is permissible, as demonstrated by the authentic prophetic narration cited above.

So, we can say that it is not forbidden to ask for help from Allah’s unseen creation, who have been given power or are close to Him. But thinking that these creatures are the source of power on their own is wrong. It is just like asking a doctor to cure us, while knowing that only Allah makes the medicine work. In the same way, if we think the doctor is a god or all-powerful, it becomes a major sin (_shirk_). But if we see the doctor as someone just using the skills given to him by Allah, it fits perfectly with this hadith.
