
He was born in Isfahan, Persia, into a Zoroastrian family, Salman spent decades searching for truth across Zoroastrianism and Christianity and became monotheist, traveling from Persia to Syria and eventually reaching Madinah, where he embraced Islam upon meeting the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ).

**Spiritual Status** — The Prophet declared "Salman is from us, the Ahlulbayt,"([[Virtues and Merits/Salman Farsi/SALMAN AND AHLULBAYT]]) granting him a rank unparalleled among non-blood companions. In Shia tradition he is counted among the Four Pillars — the most steadfast supporters of Imam Ali (ع).

[[The Battle of Trench (627 CE)]] — His most famous contribution was proposing the strategy of digging a defensive trench around Madinah in 627 CE, a Persian military tactic that successfully protected the Muslim community from a coalition army of 10,000.

**Character** — He served as Governor of Madina yet lived like a poor man, donating his salary and weaving baskets for personal income. Imam Ali (ع) compared his wisdom to that of Luqman the Wise.
