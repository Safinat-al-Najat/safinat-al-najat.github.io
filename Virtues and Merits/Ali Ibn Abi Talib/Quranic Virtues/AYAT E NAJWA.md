Were you dismayed by having to offer charities before your private talks? Since you did not do it, and Allah has excused you [for not be able to comply], now maintain the prayer and pay the zakat, and obey Allah and His Apostle. Allah is well aware of what you do.

[Quran 58:13](https://thaqalayn.net/quran/58/13)

Below is the hadith that confirms it.

"Hazrat Ali ibn Abi Talib (May Allah be pleased with him) states: 'The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "In the Holy Quran, there is a verse (the Verse of Najwa) upon which no one acted before me, and no one will act upon it after me." That verse is: **{O you who have believed, when you wish to private consult the Messenger, offer something in charity before your consultation.}** ([Al-Mujadila: 12](https://quran.com/58/13))

(Translation from Kanz-ul-Iman by Imam Ahmad Raza Khan): "O believers! When you wish to speak privately with the Messenger, give some charity before your request."

Hazrat Ali (R.A) continues: "I had one dinar, which I exchanged for ten dirhams. Whenever I wished to request something in the service of the Holy Prophet ﷺ, I would offer one dirham in charity before making my request. Then this verse was abrogated (cancelled), and no one else was able to act upon it. Subsequently, this verse was revealed: **{Have you feared to present charities before your consultation? Since you did not do so and Allah has forgiven you, then establish prayer and give zakah and obey Allah and His Messenger. And Allah is Acquainted with what you do.}** (Al-Mujadila: 13)"

(Translation from Kanz-ul-Iman by Imam Ahmad Raza Khan): "Were you afraid that you would have to give some charity before your request? Then, when you did not do so and Allah turned to you with His mercy, keep up the prayer and give zakat and remain obedient to Allah and His Messenger; and Allah knows your deeds."

**Note:** This Hadith is authentic (Sahih) according to the standards of Imam Bukhari and Imam Muslim, though the two Sheikhs (Bukhari and Muslim) did not include it in their specific collections.


[Mustadrak Al Hakim 3794](https://quranohadith.com/mustadrak/3794)

It has been cross graded by Dhahabi who is very strict in ranking a hadith that explains the virtues of Ali.

[Tafsir by Ibn Kathir](https://www.alim.org/quran/tafsir/ibn-kathir/surah/58/12/) also reports this event.
