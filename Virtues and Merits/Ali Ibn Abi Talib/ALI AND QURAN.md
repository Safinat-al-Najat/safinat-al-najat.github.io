Thabit, the freed slave of Abu Dharr

**Core Text:**

> Thabit, the freed slave of Abu Dharr, said: "I was with Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, on the Day of the Camel. When I saw Aisha standing [there], some of the doubts that enter people's minds entered mine. However, Allah removed that from me at the time of the Zuhr prayer, so I fought alongside the Commander of the Faithful.
> 
> When the battle finished, I went to Medina and visited Umm Salamah. I said: 'By Allah, I did not come to ask for food or drink, but I am the freed slave of Abu Dharr.' She said: 'Welcome.' So I narrated my story to her, and she asked: 'Where were you when hearts were flying out of terror?' I replied: 'To the point where Allah removed that [doubt] from me when the sun began to decline.' She said: 'You did well. I heard the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, say: _Ali is with the Quran, and the Quran is with Ali; they will never separate until they meet me at the Basin (Al-Hawd)._'"

**Author's Comment:**

> "This is a hadith with an authentic chain of transmission (_sahih al-isnad_), and Abu Sa'id al-Taymi is [also known as] 'Uqaysha, who is trustworthy and reliable, but they [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] did not record it."
