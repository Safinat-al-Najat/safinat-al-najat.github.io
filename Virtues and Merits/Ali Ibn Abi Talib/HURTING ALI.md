**Narrated by Amr bin Shas al-Aslami (who was among the companions of al-Hudaybiyah):**

> "We went out with Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, to Yemen, and he treated me harshly during that journey until I felt some resentment in my heart. When I returned, I openly expressed my complaint about him in the mosque until that reached the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him.
> 
> One morning, I entered the mosque while the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, was among a group of his companions. When he saw me, he fixed his eyes upon me—staring intently at me—until I sat down. He then said: 'O Amr, by Allah, you have surely hurt me.' I said: 'I seek refuge with Allah from hurting you, O Messenger of Allah!' He said: 'Yes, whoever hurts Ali has surely hurt me.'"
> 
> **Author's Comment:** This is a hadith with an authentic chain of transmission (_sahih al-isnad_), but they [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] did not record it.
