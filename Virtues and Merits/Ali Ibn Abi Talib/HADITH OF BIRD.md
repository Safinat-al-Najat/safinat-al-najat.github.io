Anas bin Malik, may Allah be pleased with him (reported by Thabit al-Bunani)

**Core Text:**

> Anas bin Malik was sick, and Muhammad bin al-Hajjaj came to visit him among his companions. They discussed Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, and Muhammad bin al-Hajjaj disparaged him. Anas said: "Sit me up! Sit me up!" They sat him up, and he said: "O Ibn al-Hajjaj! I see you disparaging Ali ibn Abi Talib, may Allah be pleased with him. By the One Who sent Muhammad, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, with the truth, I was the servant of the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, serving him daily. Every day, a youth from the Ansar would serve the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him. On my designated day, Umm Ayman came with a bird and placed it before the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him. He said: 'O Umm Ayman, what is this bird?' She replied: 'This is a bird I caught, so I prepared it for you.'
> 
> The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said: 'O Allah, bring to me the most beloved of Your creation to You and to me, to eat with me from this bird.'
> 
> Anas said: 'O Allah, make it a man from the Ansar.' Then someone knocked at the door. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said: 'O Anas, look who is at the door.' I went, and it was Ali at the door. I said: 'The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, is busy with something.' So I returned to my spot.
> 
> Then someone knocked the door a second time. The Prophet said: 'O Anas, look who is at the door.' I said: 'O Allah, make it a man from the Ansar.' I went, and it was Ali at the door. I said: 'The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, is busy with something.' So I returned to my spot.
> 
> Then someone knocked the door a third time. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said: 'O Anas, go open the door for him and let him enter. You are not the first one to love his own people, for he is not from the Ansar.' So I went and let him enter. He placed the bird before the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, and they both ate from it completely.
> 
> Muhammad bin al-Hajjaj asked: 'O Anas, did this happen in your presence?' Anas replied: 'Yes.' He said: 'I swear by Allah that I will never disparage Ali after my stance today, nor do I know anyone who disparages him except that his face is altered [with signs of hypocrisy].'"
