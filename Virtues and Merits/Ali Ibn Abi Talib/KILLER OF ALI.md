Ammar bin Yasir narrated:

> "I and Ali were companions in the Expedition of Al-Ushayrah. When the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, arrived there and stayed, we saw some people from the tribe of Bani Mudlij working at a spring of theirs in a palm grove. Ali said to me, 'O Abu Al-Yaqdhan, shall we go to these people and see how they work?' So we went to them and watched their work for an hour. Then we were overcome by sleep, so I and Ali went and lay down on some mounds of fine dust under small palm trees, and we slept. By Allah, nothing woke us up except the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, nudging us with his foot. We had become covered in dust from that mound. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said to Ali, 'What is the matter with you, O Abu Turab (Father of Dust)?' as he saw the dust on him. Then he said, 'Shall I not inform you of the two most wretched of men?' We said, 'Yes, O Messenger of Allah.' He said, 'The fair-skinned one of Thamud who hamstrung the she-camel, and the one who will strike you, O Ali, upon this—meaning his head—until this—meaning his beard—is soaked with blood.'"
> 
> **Author's Comment:** This is an authentic (sahih) hadith according to the criteria of Muslim, but they Al-Bukhari and Muslim did not record it with this additional wording. They only agreed upon the hadith of Abu Hazim from Sahl bin Sa'd: "Get up, O Abu Turab."



[Mustadrak Al Hakim 4679](https://quranohadith.com/mustadrak/4679)

Killer of Ali is the most wretched man of the whole mankind.
