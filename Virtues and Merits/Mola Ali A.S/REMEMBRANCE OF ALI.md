**Prophetic Traditions:**

> The Prophet, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said:  
> "Looking at the face of Ali is an act of worship."

**Narration of Mu'adh bin Jabal:**

> Abu Hurayrah narrated: "I saw Mu'adh bin Jabal constantly staring at Ali ibn Abi Talib. So I asked him, 'Why do you keep staring at Ali as if you have never seen him before?' He replied, 'I heard the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, say: _Looking at the face of Ali is an act of worship._'"

**Critical Scholarly Comment:**

> **Al-Khatib al-Baghdadi said:** "This hadith with this specific chain of transmission is false (_batil_). We do not know that Muhammad bin Ayyub ever narrated anything from Hawdhah bin Khalifah, nor did he ever hear from him. This is because Hawdhah died in the year 216 AH, whereas Muhammad bin Ayyub did not begin seeking hadith until the year 220 AH."

**Prophetic Traditions:**

> Abu Dharr narrated that the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said:  
> "The parable of Ali among you—or he said: in this nation—is like the covered Kaaba; looking at it is an act of worship, and making pilgrimage to it is a mandatory obligation."

**Scholarly Commentary:**

> **Abu Sulayman al-Khattabi said:** "Its meaning—and Allah knows best—is that looking at his face invites the remembrance of Allah, because of the light of Islam that can be seen upon him, the radiance of faith, and what is evident upon him from the marks of prostration and the signs of deep humility."

**Final Prophetic Tradition:**

> Aisha narrated that the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said:  
> "Mentioning Ali is an act of worship."

Although there is a big difference in opinion, yet some of sunni scholars considers it as Authentic as Imam Al Hakim, Al Suyuti and Ibn Asakir. While Majority regard it as daif and some regard it as fabricated.

The one who support it says that if you mention qualities of a Righteous person, it pleases Allah. In Quran Allah has mentioned numerous of his righteous people. So, even it falls in daif category, but according to it's matan it can be considered as correct.
