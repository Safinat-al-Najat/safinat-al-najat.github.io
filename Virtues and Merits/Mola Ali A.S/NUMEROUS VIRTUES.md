Amr bin Maymun narrated:

> "I was sitting with Ibn Abbas when a group of nine people came to him and said: 'O Ibn Abbas, either come with us or let us talk in private apart from these people.' Ibn Abbas said: 'Rather, I will go with you.' At that time, he was still healthy before he lost his sight. They began talking, and we did not know what they said.
> 
> Then Ibn Abbas came back shaking his clothes and saying: 'Fie upon them! They are attacking a man who possesses ten virtues that no one else has!
> 
> **1.** They are speaking ill of a man to whom the Prophet, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said on the day of Khaybar: _"I will certainly send a man whom Allah will never disgrace; he loves Allah and His Messenger, and Allah and His Messenger love him."_ Everyone craned their necks to see, but he said: _"Where is Ali?"_ They said: _"He is at the hand-mill grinding."_ He said: _"Could none of them grind for him?"_ Then Ali came, suffering from sore eyes, almost unable to see. The Prophet blew into his eyes, then waved the flag three times and gave it to him, and Ali returned with Safiyyah bint Huyayy.'
> 
> **2.** Ibn Abbas continued: 'Then the Prophet sent someone with Surah at-Tawbah, but then he sent Ali after him to take it from him, saying: _"No one should convey it except a man who is from me and I am from him."_'
> 
> **3.** Ibn Abbas said: 'And the Prophet, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said to his paternal cousins: _"Which of you will be my ally in this world and the Hereafter?"_ They refused, but Ali sat with them and said: _"I will be your ally in this world and the Hereafter."_ The Prophet said: _"You are my ally in this world and the Hereafter."_'
> 
> **4.** Ibn Abbas said: 'And Ali was the first of mankind to believe after Khadijah, may Allah be pleased with her.'
> 
> **5.** Ibn Abbas said: 'The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, took his cloak and placed it over Ali, Fatima, Hasan, and Husayn, and said: _“Allah intends only to remove from you the impurity of sin, O people of the Prophet's household, and to purify you with a thorough purification.”_ [Quran 33:33]'
> 
> **6.** Ibn Abbas said: 'And Ali risked his life by wearing the garment of the Prophet, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, and sleeping in his place. The polytheists were throwing stones at him, believing he was the Prophet. Abu Bakr came while Ali was sleeping and called out, "O Prophet of Allah!" Ali replied, "The Prophet of Allah has gone toward the Well of Maymun, go catch up with him." So Abu Bakr left and entered the cave with him. Ali spent the night being pelted with stones as the Prophet used to be, writhing in pain but wrapping his head in the garment, not uncovering it until morning. They then uncovered his head and said, "You are a coward; your companion would not cover his head and we pelted him while he did not flinch, but you flinched." We strongly denounced that claim.'
> 
> **7.** Ibn Abbas said: 'The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, went out for the expedition of Tabuk, and the people went out with him. Ali said to him: "Shall I go out with you?" The Prophet said: "No." Ali wept, so the Prophet said to him: "Are you not pleased that you are to me as Aaron was to Moses, except that there is no prophet after me? Indeed, it is not fitting that I should depart unless you are my successor (caliph)."'
> 
> **8.** Ibn Abbas said: 'The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said to him: "You are the guardian (wali) of every believer after me."'
> 
> **9.** Ibn Abbas said: 'The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, closed all the doors leading into the mosque except the door of Ali. He used to enter the mosque while in a state of major ritual impurity (janabah), as it was his only path.'
> 
> **10.** Ibn Abbas continued: 'And the Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said: _"Whomever I am his master (mawla), then Ali is his master."_'
> 
> Ibn Abbas said: 'Allah, the Mighty and Majestic, has informed us in the Quran that He was pleased with the companions of the tree (those who gave the Pledge of Ridwan), for He knew what was in their hearts. Did He ever inform us that He became angry with them after that?'
> 
> Ibn Abbas said: 'And the Prophet of Allah, peace and blessings of Allah be upon him, said to Umar, may Allah be pleased with him, when Umar asked, "Permit me to strike his neck": "Would you really do that? And what makes you know? Perhaps Allah looked upon the people of Badr and said: Do whatever you wish."'
