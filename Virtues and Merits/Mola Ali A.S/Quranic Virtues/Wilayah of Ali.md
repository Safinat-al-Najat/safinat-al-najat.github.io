[Quran 5:55](https://quran.com/5/55)

Although, there is difference in opinion in translation of this verse but yet some do write it in their tafseers while some don't.

![[image-3.png]]

First Highlighted Section (Top)

**Main Narrator:** Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with them both (reported by Imam Al-Khatib in _Al-Muttafiq_)

**Core Text:**

> "Imam Al-Khatib has narrated a report in _Al-Muttafiq_ from Hazrat Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with them both, that Hazrat Ali, the Lion of God, may Allah be pleased with him, gave his ring as charity while he was in the position of bowing (_ruku_). The Holy Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, asked the beggar, 'Who gave you this ring?' He replied, 'This person who is bowing.' Thereupon, Allah the Almighty revealed this verse."

---

Second Highlighted Section (Middle)

**Main Narrator:** Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with them both (reported by multiple Imams)

**Core Text:**

> "Imam Abd al-Razzaq, Abd bin Humayd, Ibn Jarir, Abu al-Shaykh, and Ibn Mardawayh have narrated a report from Hazrat Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with them both, that this verse was revealed in favor of Hazrat Ali bin Abi Talib, may Allah be pleased with him."

---

Third Highlighted Section (Bottom)

**Main Narrator:** Ammar bin Yasir, may Allah be pleased with him (reported by Imam Al-Tabarani and Ibn Mardawayh)

**Core Text:**

> "Imam Al-Tabarani in _Al-Awsat_ and Ibn Mardawayh have narrated a report from Hazrat Ammar bin Yasir, may Allah be pleased with him, that a beggar came and stood near Hazrat Ali, the Lion of God, may Allah be pleased with him, while he was performing a voluntary (_nafl_) prayer. Hazrat Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, took off his ring and gave it to the beggar. The beggar then came to the presence of the Holy Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, and narrated the entire incident. Consequently, this verse was revealed to the Holy Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him. The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, recited this verse to his companions and then said: 'Whomever I am his friend (_mawla_), Ali is his friend. O Allah, be a friend to whoever extends friendship to Hazrat Ali, and take as an enemy whoever holds enmity toward Ali.'"
