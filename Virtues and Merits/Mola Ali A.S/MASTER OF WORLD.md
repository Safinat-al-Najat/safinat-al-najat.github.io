"Hazrat Abdullah bin Abbas (may Allah be pleased with him) said: The Prophet (peace and blessings of Allah be upon him) looked at (Hazrat Ali) and said: O Ali! You are the leader in this world and the Hereafter. Your friend is my friend and my friend is the friend of Allah and your enemy is my enemy, the enemy of Allah and woe to him who hates you after me."

This hadith is authentic according to the criteria of Imam Bukhari (may Allah have mercy on him) and Imam Muslim (may Allah have mercy on him), but the two Sheikhs (may Allah have mercy on him) did not narrate it. And all the scholars of hadith are unanimous that Abu Al-Azhar is trustworthy. And according to the law of the scholars of hadith, when a trustworthy person is single, the hadith he narrates is authentic. Abu Abdullah Al-Qurashi narrates: Ahmad bin Yahya Al-Halwani says: When Abu Al-Azhar came from Sana'a and there was a discussion with the people of Baghdad about this hadith, Yahya bin Mu'in rejected it. One day, at the end of his lecture, he said: Where is that liar from Nishapur? This hadith was narrated by Abdur Razzaq. He narrates in this manner. So Abu al-Azhar stood up and said: I am that person. Yahya bin Mu'in laughed at his standing up in such a crowded gathering and his blatant confession. Then he drew him close to him and said: How is it that Abdul Razzaq narrated this hadith only to you and not to anyone else? He said: O Abu Zakariyya! You should know that when I reached Sana'a, Abdul Razzaq had gone to a town far away. I set out (to meet him) although my health was also bad at that time. When I reached him, he asked me about the news of Khorasan, I narrated it to him, then I wrote down the hadith from him and set off for Sana'a with him. When I started to leave him, he said to me: You have a right on me. I will narrate a hadith to you, which no one has heard from me until today except you, by God. "By Allah! Then he narrated the same hadith to me word for word. So Yahya bin Mu'in accepted what he said and retracted his words."

[Mustadrak Al Hakim 4640](https://quranohadith.com/mustadrak/4640)

According to this hadith, Ali is the master of this world and hereafter.
